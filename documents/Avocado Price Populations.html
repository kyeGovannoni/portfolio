Estimating the true population mean AveragePrice¶

$ \mu_\bar{X} =\mu $

$\sigma_\bar{X} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$

We dont know the population standard devition but we can estimate it given n is large enough.

$\sigma_\bar{X} \approx \frac{S}{\sqrt{n}} $

99% of all the means are within $3\sigma$ of $ \mu_\bar{X}$ or $\mu $

#Sample means 
means =[round(samples[s].mean(),2) for s in samples.keys()]
n = [samples[s].shape[0] for s in samples.keys()]
sample_std = [round(np.sqrt(((samples[s]-samples[s].mean())**2).sum()/(samples[s].shape[0]-1)),3) for s in samples.keys()]

values = list(zip(means,sample_std,n))
sample_statistics = pd.DataFrame(data = values,index=samples.keys(),columns=['xbar','s','n'])
