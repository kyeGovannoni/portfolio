Estimating E(X) AveragePrice for sub populations organic and conventional¶

$ \mu_\bar{X} =E(X) $

$\sigma_\bar{X} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$

We dont know the population standard devition but we can estimate it given n is large enough.

$\sigma_\bar{X} \approx \frac{S}{\sqrt{n}} $

99% of all the means are within $3\sigma$ of $ \mu_\bar{X}$ or $E(X)$

#sample 1 statistics.
x1 = _2015_sample.append(_2015_sample.append(_2017_sample.append(_2018_sample)))
x1_mean = x1.mean()
x1_n = x1.shape[0]
s1 = np.sqrt(((x1 - x1_mean)**2).sum()/(x1_n - 1))
#sample1 estimation
est_std_xbar = s1/(np.sqrt(x1_n))
_99confidance = est_std_xbar*3

#sample 2 statistics.
x2 = _2015_sample2.append(_2015_sample2.append(_2017_sample2.append(_2018_sample2)))
